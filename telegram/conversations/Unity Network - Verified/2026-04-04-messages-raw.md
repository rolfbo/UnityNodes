Crg878, [Apr 4, 2026 at 11:45]
Just my thoughts on calibration, etc. I’m no expert, but I can’t relate to some of the development in things I’ve done in the past. Like many others, I saw Unity as an exciting new project with the potential for a good part-time income, who hasn’t looked for something like that before! It felt perfect.
After some research, I realised it was still early days. It would be an investment and a bit of a gamble, with no guarantee of success. I knew I’d be part of Phase 1, providing numbers to help the network grow. At this stage, there’s limited data  and the focus is simply on getting the  network up and running. It doesn’t matter too much who or what is running the licences.
Think of it like Wacky Races, you can turn up in anything,  a rocket car or an old wooden box. If you can get it working and keep it running, you’re in the race.
Once enough numbers are reached, Phase 2 begins, quality. With real world data, the network matures and things start taking shape. Different setups emerge, big farms, micro setups, some more effective than others. It’s a bit like the Wild West, with all kinds of approaches. At this stage, it’s about refining and improving.
I see rewards like In the race you also collected coins,  the fastest, most reliable setups get the big gold coins, while slower ones pick up smaller rewards. Even a basic setup can still contribute by collecting the smaller coins that others overlook, still valuable, just at a different level.
Everyone will contribute differently and have different value. The top “rocket car” setups will likely perform best, influenced by factors like location as well as hardware. At the other end, simpler setups may struggle but still contribute over time, with many levels in between.
Once the right tools are in place, we can optimise our setups and aim to move further up the field picking up more of the bigger rewards.
The next phase is rolling out tasks. The network needs to be reliable and stable, and this will be an ongoing process as it grows and matures.
