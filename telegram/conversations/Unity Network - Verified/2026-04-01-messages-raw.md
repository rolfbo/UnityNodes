Unity Network Assistant, [Apr 1, 2026 at 03:13]
The network is still recalibrating following a major cleanup of synthetic devices, VPNs, emulators and other setups with poor telemetry commercial value. 10 or 20 phones per network is within the recommended range, but rewards also depend on your overall network setup, device types and the tasks currently running. 

Not all setups generate the same value for telemetry customers - but that doesn’t mean they’re without value. Different task types serve different customers, and as more tasks come online, different setups will be utilised in different ways.

What underperforms for telemetry could be exactly what’s needed for another task type.

Also - are those your own devices or ULOs you’re managing? If they’re ULOs, you may not have full visibility into their actual setups. They could be on low quality networks, running far more than 10 or 20 phones per network, or they could all be clustered on a single network. 

The ULO Network Evaluation Toolkit announced today will give you the ability to evaluate exactly that - device grading, network quality, connectivity and more - so you can see at a glance which ULOs are performing well and which setups might be dragging your rewards down.

New task types are also rolling out now and in the coming days, including a WiFi-only task designed for a wider range of network configurations. 

The tools and the tasks to fix this are on the way and network recalibration is ​​​​​​​​​​​in progress.
