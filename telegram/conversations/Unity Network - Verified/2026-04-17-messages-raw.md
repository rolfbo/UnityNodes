I follow the chats regularly, and I’ll be honest — it can feel quite depressing at times.
Coming from a technical background myself, I’m very familiar with deadlines and high expectations. In my experience, the best antidote to missed targets is **transparency**. Unfortunately, with external dependencies and tight timelines, delivering that transparency gets increasingly difficult.
At this point, we really have two paths forward:
We can either keep trusting the team to deliver (with roughly the same level of trust we had when we bought our nodes), **or** we can work together to build a shared understanding of what “transparency” actually means for everyone here.

One thing I’ve observed is that we should set **different expectations** for different types of work. For example, it’s reasonable to expect relatively accurate delivery dates on things like UI improvements, because the team has full control over them (assuming they’re not relying on a third party).
On the other hand, tasks with many moving parts — like the CLI work — are much harder to pin down precisely. I guess that the Unity development piece was completed before the partners and external dependencies were fully cleared. So the team can release their part, but other pieces are still pending.

Since we have a strong group of developers in this chat, I think it would be valuable to create a simple **development tracker dashboard** (similar to the Cardano Leios one). If the team is open to it, they could give us periodic percentage-based updates on each major task. That alone would help align expectations and cut down on repetitive “Wen, when?” questions. Once a task is at 40 %, there’s no need to ask for daily updates.
