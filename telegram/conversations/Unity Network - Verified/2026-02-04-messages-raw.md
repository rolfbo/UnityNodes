Mat, [Feb 4, 2026 at 05:01]
Nothing beyond setting power management to not stop the app and also locking it so it's not closed in a close all action. 
main point is it only works well when plugged in all the time.

Specifics:
1. Disable Battery Optimization for Apps 
This prevents Android from automatically shutting down apps in the background. 
Go to Settings > Battery.
Tap on More settings or Battery usage.
Select the app you want to keep running.
Choose Don't optimize or set to Unrestricted. 

2. Lock Apps in the Recent Apps Screen
Locking an app ensures it stays in memory and isn't closed when you press "Close All". 
Open the app you want to keep running.
Open the Recent Apps screen (swipe up and hold, or press the button with three vertical lines).
Tap the two dots/menu icon in the top right corner of the app card.
Select Lock. A lock icon will appear on the app. 

3. Disable Automatic App Launch (If apps are crashing/restarting) 
Oppo's ColorOS may be preventing apps from auto-starting. 
Go to Settings > Apps > Auto-launch.
Toggle OFF the restriction for apps you want to keep running.
