SoC stands for "System on a Chip" - You can think of it as whole computer on a single chip.

TEE stands for "Trusted Execution Environment" - It's a dedicated "piece" of the SoC that's isolated from the rest of the "computer" - it runs it's own operating system, and acts as a kind of "vault" where communications between it and the rest of the "computer" are tightly controlled and restricted.

A popular example of a TEE is Apple's Secure Enclave

- API - for developers building Unity Network's functionality into their own platforms

More demand flowing through the network means more tasks, which means more reward potential. Higher value tasks earn proportionally higher rewards. The more consistently your device is online, the higher your reputation score, and the more premium work you receive.
